About

The People's President of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as the Missile Man of India, was a legendary aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Born in a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam rose through sheer determination and brilliance to become one of India's most celebrated scientists and statesmen. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Even after his presidency, he dedicated his life to inspiring the youth of India, teaching at universities and delivering lectures across the country until his very last day — he passed away while doing what he loved most: teaching students.

Life Journey

A Life of Purpose

1931

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

Born into a modest family, Kalam delivered newspapers to support his family's income from a young age.

1954

Graduated in Physics — St. Joseph's College

Completed his physics degree, then went on to study aerospace engineering at MIT, Chennai.

1969

Joined ISRO — India's Space Program

Became project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.

1983

Directed India's Missile Development — DRDO

Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), developing missiles like Agni and Prithvi — earning him the title "Missile Man of India."

1998

Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests

Played a key role in India's second successful nuclear tests, establishing India as a nuclear power.

2002

Elected 11th President of India

Became the People's President, known for his accessibility, humility, and deep connection with the youth of India.

2015

Passed Away While Teaching

Collapsed during a lecture at IIM Shillong on July 27, 2015. He died doing what he loved — inspiring young minds.

Legacy

A Legacy That Lives On

Dr. Kalam's legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians and people worldwide. His vision of a developed India by 2020, his passion for education, and his humble humanity remain timeless.

🚀 Missile Man of India

Transformed India's defence capabilities with Agni, Prithvi, Akash, Trishul and Nag missile systems.

🛸 Father of India's Space Program

His work at ISRO laid the foundation for India becoming a global space power.

📖 Author & Teacher

Wrote iconic books like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020" that inspired millions.

❤️ People's President

Known for visiting remote villages, meeting children, and being the most accessible President India ever had.
